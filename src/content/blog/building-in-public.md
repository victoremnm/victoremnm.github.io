There's something powerful about building in public. It keeps you accountable, creates opportunities for feedback, and connects you with people on similar journeys.

## The Fear of Sharing Unfinished Work

Most of us were taught to only share polished, final results. But this misses the value of the process itself.

When you share works-in-progress:

1. **You get early feedback** — catch mistakes before they compound
2. **You build an audience** — people love following journeys
3. **You stay accountable** — public commitments are harder to break

## How I Approach It

I try to share:

- Weekly updates on what I'm building
- Technical decisions and their reasoning
- Failures and what I learned from them

## The Uncomfortable Truth

Building in public is scary. You're exposing your unfinished work, your mistakes, your learning process. But that vulnerability is exactly what makes it valuable.

Give it a try. Start small. Share something you're working on, even if it's not perfect yet.
