Something that has given me quite a bit of anxiety for a while was what's my end game for my parents. I thought and worried about this, thinking that I am not interested in giving them the same type of care that they gave for our grandparents. I feel guilty that I can't see myself sacrificing my career and goals to take care of them. That's all I really remembered during my adolescence, and I want none of it.

And then, today, I realized that I don't have to follow their path. I have a better idea, albeit a very reductionist approach. The reductionist and rationalist perspective gives very objective sanity on something as nebulous as solving the parent-child, filial piety problem.

## The Plan

From a long term perspective, I see myself living on the West Coast, either the Bay Area or SoCal. Being in either of these locations gives me the viability to visit my parents easily, either a short cheap flight from SFO/SJC to SAN, or a 2-hour drive from LA. Given this ease, I plan to visit them once a month. I am to do so, but a little less is okay. My sister will swap every two weeks with me. As a result, they will only miss either child every two weeks. We will visit them for a total of 24 weekends. I get to visit San Diego and hang out with my friends but giving them quality time.

When I see them, I will be sure to spend quality time with them in the following ways:

- If I'm in San Diego, we will do a combination of going on hikes or walks. We will do some classic things like go to the Zoo or museums. I will also open them to check out other things like getting some dinner at Little Italy or see a show in La Jolla Playhouse, exposing them to other sides of San Diego that they haven't seen. Of course, we will have a home-cooked meal before I leave back to my city.
- These visits won't have to be in San Diego. They can visit me in SF or LA. Either is fine. I should have a place by then.
- We can opt to rendezvous at a travel location. That's still quality time. No more cheap AYCE cruises. We can actually fly out to other places, probably once a year. We still need to plan out that trip to Cambodia. Hopefully, we can go in 2021.

## The Timeline

Eventually, I will have a family, I will have a wife, I will have children, and possibly a dog or cat. They can be grandparents now, yay! They can now be caretakers while I still grind and work. Also, when I have a family, I plan to move back to San Diego. The schooling is great there, and I want my children to experience the beauty of that city.

Given this plan, I ask myself what is the time table? What are the timelines that come to mind? My grandma passed away at age 89 when I was 24. That gives me the due date of having a child when my parents turn 65. That is another 10 years to achieve this plan: get married, get a home, get a child, all the fixings.

Even after 10 years, that is also around another 24 years of life that they still have until they pass away. That is enough time to achieve my goals of paying back my parents for everything they've done for me. By the time that they get older, we will live near, and I can routinely check in with them. I will even pay for their caretaker, expenses all figured out. No worries.

## The Freedom

And that gives me a ton of freedom to think and explore as a 28-year-old before I turn 38. I probably won't even have to start thinking about my spouse or marrying until 5 years later, which is when I turn 33. That also gives me 5 years to live like a young newlywed couple doing newlywed things before 38.

This gives me around 2 years to court that person that I would want to marry in full. This gives me 3 years of unfiltered exploration. This gives me 3 years to live in a different city, possibly residing outside America or traveling nomadically. That gives me 3 years to continue grinding on my job, giving me the likelihood of making a manager or higher staff position by that time to care for my family. That gives me 3 years to explore my other passions in full fervor, possibly in sports or art. That is enough time to get things right and achieve what I want to achieve.

## The Caveat

Okay, but this is assuming all conditions hold such as my parents living up until 89 and me residing on the west coast in 10 years. Of course, there should be flexibility with my plan. There are chances that I won't get married at that time, if at all. But there are chances that I pour my soul into a different city, work, or art; and I happily invite all of that if I am lucky. Who knows? It's just a plan, and I believe it has the fairest likelihood given how I'm shaping my life is trending right now. Also, my sister is here, so we can double up on this plan that doesn't seem overly obtrusive.

The above plan I outlined is a fallback plan, nothing more, or a base plan, whichever. So I guess, what I'm trying to say is that I have time, not infinite, but enough. I am writing to myself not to worry. These are all possible milestones to accomplish with the allotted time.
